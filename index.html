🔍 Insight 6 — Did Post's Coalition Transfer to Browning?

In 2024, Karen Post's strongest early voting counties (by share) were Cochran, Bailey, Gray, Wheeler, Hale, Castro, and Hockley — counties where she exceeded 25% of the early vote. If Browning is consolidating the same anti-King geography, those counties should be up or holding. Here's what actually happened:

🎯 Summary Scorecard — What the Early Vote Tells Us
KING STRENGTHS: Home county (Hemphill) tripled EV. His core base counties — Andrews, Gray, Donley, Collingsworth, Briscoe — held or grew. The late-week surge in Gray, Andrews, and Hockley signals organized GOTEV effort. Hall County's surge (+122%) is a wild card that, given King's 80.5% share there in 2024, likely benefits him.
KING CONCERNS: Gaines (−71%), Yoakum (−33%), Childress (−48%), and Lamb (−36%) represent significant dropout from counties he dominated in 2024. These are not swing counties — they're base counties going quiet. Total district EV is down 18.5%, meaning fewer absolute votes to work with on Election Day.
BROWNING CONCERNS: His home county (Hale) has lost nearly 1,000 early votes vs. 2024's challenger (also from Hale). Post's anti-King coalition has not transferred. Cochran, Wheeler, and Hale — the three highest Post-share counties in 2024 — are all down sharply. Browning's EV footprint is not visible in the geography the way Post's was.
WATCH ON ELECTION DAY: Gray, Hockley, and Andrews represent 41.5% of 2026 early votes and will likely be decisive. Gaines and Hale County Election Day patterns will reveal whether the early vote suppression reflects disengagement or a shift to ED voting. Hall County's unexplained surge should be monitored for a down-ballot race effect.
